Dana Forry

Program Director


Dana Forry

dforry@ryasap.org

      Dana Forry is the Program Director of the RYASAP Juvenile Review Board (JRB) in Bridgeport and has held this position since 2012. She holds a BA in Forensic Psychology from UCONN and a MA in Community Psychology, concentration in Forensic Psychology from the University of New Haven. Dana began at RYASAP in 2009 and served as a JRB intern and Case Manager prior to assuming the role of Program Director. Over the years, Dana has established and maintained community relationships to ensure usage of the JRB and has been a member of the Greater Bridgeport LIST (Local Interagency Strategic Team) since 2011 serving as co-facilitator from 2014-2020. She is also an active participant on Bridgeport’s committee to reducing Racial and Ethnic Disparities (RED) in the juvenile justice system, the statewide Diversion Workgroup of the JJPOC (Juvenile Justice Policy and Oversight Committee) and advocates tirelessly about the importance of diversionary options for youth. Dana is a trainer in Balanced and Restorative Justice and her work also includes serving as a community volunteer panel member for the Stratford and Trumbull JRBs and assisting them with training and technical assistance as needed. Dana’s dedication to helping troubled youth has made her a champion of the JRB and restorative justice models for helping at risk juveniles get back on the right track.
