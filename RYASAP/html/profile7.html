Lisa McKenna

Program Manager


Lisa McKenna

lmckenna@ryasap.org

      "Lisa McKenna is currently responsible for delivering RYASAP’s Juvenile Court-Based Mediation Program in Bridgeport, Danbury, Stamford, Torrington and Waterbury. With over 30 years of experience in the field of conflict resolution, Lisa has been an integral member of RYASAP’s Mediation Program since 2002, which formerly operated under the Dispute Settlement Center (DSC).

Lisa focuses on key elements such as quality assurance, program effectiveness, and site protocols in her approach to mediation. She has developed excellent relationships within each of the courts and understands how mediation fits within the justice system as a compliment to existing services.

She previously supervised staff and ensured program delivery of the agency’s Bridgeport, Norwalk and Waterbury, Adult Court Support Services funded mediation work. In addition to her work in the mediation program, Lisa was part of the design team for RYASAP’s StreetSafe Bridgeport program; a gang and gun diversion initiative. In that role, she developed and implemented best practices for the launch of the program. Additionally, she served as both a mediator and Program manager in the Bridgeport and Waterbury GA courts.

Prior to her work with RYASAP and DS,C Lisa was an Investigative Counselor for the New Jersey Crimes Compensation Board, as well as a mediator and coordinator for The Dispute Resolution Center of Staten Island, New York (currently operating under the New York Center for Interpersonal Development). She holds a BA in Criminal Justice from John Jay College in New York City in addition to Forensic Psychology studies at a master’s level at the college."
