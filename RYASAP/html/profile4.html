Ashley Blanchard, MSW

Director of Research and Evaluation


Ashley Blanchard, MSW

ablanchard@ryasap.org

      Ashley R. Blanchard, MSW, is the director of research and evaluation for RYASAP in Bridgeport, Connecticut where she leverages data to inform practice and facilitates evaluation processes to aid community improvement efforts. Ms. Blanchard has a passion for aiding in the eradication of systematic and structural oppression, international social work, and is focused on multi-level interventions through community-based participatory research. Ashley has been published across several mediums on social justice, youth homelessness, and socioeconomic indicators of wellbeing for children and families. She has led presentations around New England on political advocacy in social work, child welfare, and has presented internationally on how to build resilient schools, homes, and communities through social and emotional learning. Ashley is also actively involved in the National Association of Social Work’s Connecticut chapter as the Chair of the Political Action for Candidate Election (PACE) Committee. Ms. Blanchard is a political social worker with a Masters in Social Work from the University of Connecticut and has a Bachelors in Social Work from Florida Gulf Coast University. She is currently a Ph.D. student at Adelphi University.
